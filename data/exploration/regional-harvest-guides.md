# Regional Harvest-Timing Guides — Survey

**Goal:** identify expert-curated regional harvest-timing references comparable in quality to the CityFruit Seattle Ripeness Guide PDF, for the metros where Forager imports tree-pin data. Each entry below was verified by fetching the actual URL during this survey (May 2026). "Verified" means the URL returned content; "unverified" or "rejected" means it 404'd, was empty, paywalled, or thinner than it looked.

---

## 1. Top picks

These are the highest-quality regional resources. They are real, citable, and have meaningful harvest-timing content.

### 1a. CityFruit — Seattle Ripeness Guides (the benchmark)
- **URL:** https://www.cityfruit.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/Ripeness-Guides-3.pdf — verified (1.6 MB PDF, content extraction blocked by size but exists).
- **Type:** PDF, ~9 cultivated species (apple, pear, plum, cherry, fig, etc., per the task brief).
- **Scope:** Seattle / Puget Sound (Western Washington).
- **Format:** Month-bracket harvest windows + ripeness indicators (color, firmness, taste, drop).
- **Authorship:** Published by CityFruit, a Seattle nonprofit (since 2008). Named contributors per task brief.
- **License:** Standard nonprofit copyright; no Creative Commons grant. Reference-only unless permission requested.
- **Quality:** Gold standard — this is the model the brief uses.

### 1b. Hidden Harvest Ottawa — Fruit Ripening Guide
- **URL:** https://ottawa.hiddenharvest.ca/fruit-ripening-guide — verified.
- **Type:** Web pages, one per species, with photos and timeline graphics.
- **Scope:** Ottawa (Zone 5a-5b); generally applicable across the lower Great Lakes / St. Lawrence.
- **Species (9):** sweet cherries, sour cherries, serviceberries, mulberries, plums, apples, pears, grapes, elderberries.
- **Format:** Calendar month ranges (e.g., "early June to late July") + visual timeline graphic; underripe / ripe / overripe indicators with color, texture, taste, sizing references; safety notes (e.g., raw elderberry toxicity).
- **Authorship:** Hidden Harvest Ottawa, with explicit reference to Not Far From the Tree. Photographers individually credited.
- **License:** "© 2026 Hidden Harvest Ottawa" — no CC. Reference / citation only.
- **Quality:** Excellent. Probably the closest analog to CityFruit's guide that we found, and its Ottawa-specific timing complements Toronto/NFFTT well for the eastern Canadian zone.

### 1c. Not Far From The Tree (Toronto) — Fruit Guides
- **URL:** https://notfarfromthetree.org/fruit/fruit-ripening-and-picking-guides/ — verified.
- **Type:** Web pages, one per species, with prose + cultural/Indigenous-language references.
- **Scope:** Toronto (Zone 6a-6b).
- **Species (16):** apple, apricot, black walnut, cherry, crabapple, elderberry, ginkgo, grape, mulberry, pawpaw, peach, pear, plum, quince, serviceberry (note: ginkgo and black walnut broaden this beyond cultivated tree fruit).
- **Format:** Narrative month ranges (e.g., apple: "late July to late October"). Less granular than Hidden Harvest's underripe/ripe/overripe split, but broader species list.
- **Authorship:** Organizational (no individual author). NFFTT is a project of MakeWay Charitable Society.
- **License:** "© Not Far From The Tree 2026" — no CC.
- **Quality:** Strong, especially for the broader species list including foragables (ginkgo, walnut, pawpaw, mulberry, serviceberry). Hidden Harvest cites NFFTT as a source — they are partly a derived work.

### 1d. Philadelphia Orchard Project — POP Harvest Guide 2025 + Fruit Harvest Calendar
- **URLs:**
  - Guide: https://www.phillyorchards.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/POP-Harvest-Guide-2025.pdf — verified (2.1 MB, 2 pages, Canva-built).
  - Calendar: https://s3-us-west-2.amazonaws.com/phillyorchards-prod/resources/POP+Fruit+Harvest+Calendar.pdf — verified (single page, 2015 vintage).
  - Plant List: linked from https://www.phillyorchards.org/resources/.
- **Scope:** Philadelphia (Zone 7a).
- **Species:** POP orchards heavily favor figs, pawpaws, persimmons, Asian pears, blackberries — a less-conventional Mid-Atlantic mix. Per their Plant List, each plant has a "harvest window" attribute.
- **Format:** Calendar / month brackets.
- **Authorship:** Phil Forsyth (POP Executive Director) is named as PDF author.
- **License:** No explicit grant; nonprofit copyright assumed.
- **Quality:** Real and citable, but the 2-page Canva format is lighter than CityFruit/Hidden Harvest. Best taken as a Mid-Atlantic species list with timing rather than a deep ripeness guide.

### 1e. New England Tree Fruit Management Guide (UMass / UVM / UMaine)
- **URL:** https://netreefruit.org/ — verified.
- **Type:** Online "living" guide (continuously updated).
- **Scope:** New England (covers Boston-region needs).
- **Species:** Apples, pears, peaches, nectarines, plums, apricots, cherries.
- **Format:** Sections on harvest timing per crop (e.g., https://netreefruit.org/apples/cider-apples/harvest); information is narrative + ripeness indicators rather than month brackets.
- **Authorship:** Jaime Piñero & Elizabeth Garofalo (UMass), Terry Bradshaw (UVM), Renae Moran (UMaine) — all named, all extension faculty.
- **License:** "© 2026 University of Massachusetts Amherst." No CC; citation acceptable.
- **Quality:** Very high — this is the most authoritative academic source for the Northeast. Stronger on commercial-orchard maturity indices (Streif index, starch-iodine, ethylene) than on month brackets, but it is the *citable* New England answer.

### 1f. LifeCycles Project (Victoria BC) — Fruit Tree Care Booklet
- **URL:** https://lifecyclesproject.ca/app/uploads/2018/12/LifeCycles-Fruit-Tree-Care-Booklet.pdf — verified (1.1 MB).
- **Scope:** Vancouver Island / coastal BC (Zone 8b-9a) — close enough to Vancouver/PNW to be regionally usable.
- **Type:** PDF booklet (binary content not extractable in this fetch but file exists).
- **Authorship:** LifeCycles Project, operating since 1994.
- **License:** Nonprofit copyright.
- **Quality:** Established organization (30+ years); booklet covers harvesting per their site description. Worth direct human review of the PDF.

### 1g. The Giving Grove — Month-by-Month Maintenance Calendar
- **URL:** https://www.givinggrove.org/management-calendar — verified.
- **Type:** Web calendar (printable PDF gated behind email signup).
- **Scope:** US-wide community-orchard network, headquartered Kansas City — *not* regionally specific (explicitly tells users "contact your local Giving Grove program" for region-specific timing).
- **Species:** apples, pears, Asian pears, peaches, cherries, figs, jujubes, pawpaws, blueberries, strawberries, raspberries, blackberries.
- **Format:** Monthly task calendar; harvest months noted (e.g., "Pristine apples in July").
- **Authorship:** The Giving Grove (org).
- **License:** "© 2019-2026 The Giving Grove."
- **Quality:** Useful as a backbone reference for multi-zone US ranges, but its lack of regional specificity is a real limitation. Best treated as a sanity-check reference, not a primary regional source.

## 4. Honest coverage assessment

Forager has 172 species, US+Canada, USDA zones 3a-11a. The expert-curated regional guides located here cover, realistically:

- **Apple, pear, plum, cherry (sweet+sour), peach, apricot, fig** — well covered. CityFruit (PNW), Hidden Harvest (E. Ontario), NFFTT (Toronto), POP (Mid-Atlantic), New England Tree Fruit Mgmt Guide (NE), Cornell Lake Ontario (NY), UCANR (CA). Multiple regional voices per species.
- **Mulberry, serviceberry, elderberry, pawpaw, persimmon, quince, crabapple** — partially covered. NFFTT and Hidden Harvest include several; POP includes pawpaw/persimmon; USDA NRCS Plant Guides cover several individually. Coverage is thinner and more anecdotal than for cultivated tree fruit.
- **Black walnut, hazelnut, chestnut, ginkgo (nut)** — sparsely covered (NFFTT touches walnut/ginkgo; OSU has nut training guides). Foragable nuts are a gap.
- **The bulk of the 172** — likely *not* covered by these guides. Most regional urban-fruit nonprofits stop at ~9-16 cultivated species. Foragables like spicebush, beach plum, autumn olive, paw paw, native plums, juneberry varieties, native viburnums, etc., are addressed only by individual USDA Plant Guides, scattered extension fact sheets, and forager books — not by the kind of consolidated regional ripeness guide CityFruit produced.

**Geographic coverage of metros in the brief:**
- Strong: Seattle (CityFruit), Toronto (NFFTT), Ottawa (Hidden Harvest), Boston/New England (NETFMG), Philadelphia (POP), Victoria/Vancouver-adjacent (LifeCycles), California / SF Bay (UCANR), NY State (Cornell).
- Weak/missing: Portland OR (PFTP unverified), NYC (no expert guide located), Atlanta (Concrete Jungle has a map but no published guide), DC, Chicago, Calgary, Edmonton, Vancouver BC proper.

**Bottom line:** Five-to-seven good regional guides exist and are citable, collectively covering the cultivated tree-fruit subset (~15-20 species) for ~6-7 of the named metros. Beyond that, the app will need to fall back to (a) USDA NRCS Plant Guides for individual native foragables, (b) academic extension publications for specific species, and (c) USA-NPN phenology observations for date-anchored timing — which is already what `data/exploration/npn-validation-NE.md` is doing. There is no single document or set of documents that comes close to covering 172 species across this geographic range; the regional guides above are best used as ground-truth anchors for the most commonly-foraged ~20 cultivated species, not as a systematic data layer.
